Educational Path

Although having a university degree is not always necessary, but completing it can be helpful when looking for work. Areas of specialization include television, radio, sound production, film or new media. There are a range of bachelor's degree courses that provide the technical skills needed for a career in video production and most courses also provide practical experience using a variety of software packages and systems. In addition, students can also learn about the history, practice and critical analysis of film. Some students also have the opportunity to work with national and international artists-in-residence working in various fields of film and screen media production.

Applying for a Job

The next step to becoming a video producer is to find a position that fits your level of expertise. For example, you might consider becoming a production assistant, an entry-level position that allows you to build experience and skills. Typical tasks include getting actors and crew ready for the day's work or communicating instructions from the producer to the rest of the team. These jobs will give you experience and help you become a video producer in the future.

Join a professional organization

Becoming a professional member of the media industry is a great way to build your network. You can also access professional development opportunities, attend networking events, and learn about job opportunities. Plus, joining a professional body comes with additional benefits, including the ability to negotiate wages and working conditions, and advocate on your behalf on policy issues relevant to the media industry.
